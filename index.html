Research

    Under the supervision of Prof. Jean-François Lalonde and Prof. Christian Gagné, my research is focused on meta-learning, and unsupervised representation (self-supervised) learning in low supervision conditions. My current research is focused on contextual self-supervised learning for quick adaptations. With my supervisors, we have proposed the idea of associative alignment at eccv-2020 to increase the learning capacity, and we proposed persistent mixture model arxiv (under review) as a representation learning approach to gain adaptivity in few-shot classification.

    I was born and raised in Band, a beautiful village near Urmia City.

Publications and preprints


Persistent mixture model networks for few-shot classification. Arman Afrasiyabi, Jean-François Lalonde, Christian Gagné.
While previous methods represent classes with a single centroid or rely on post hoc clustering, we introduce Persistent Mixture Model (PMM) networks for learning mixture models for base classes jointly with the data representation in an end-to-end manner.

paper (under review)


Associative alignment for few-shot image classification.
Arman Afrasiyabi, Jean-François Lalonde, Christian Gagné, in European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV) 2020, got accepted as a spotlight (5% acceptance rate) presentation.
We propose associative alignment approach for leveraging part of the base data by aligning the novel training instances to the closely related ones in the base dataset.
paper | webpage (video/code)


Non-euclidean vector product for neural networks.
Arman Afrasiyabi, Diaa Badawi, Baris Nasir, Ozan Yildiz, Fatos T. Yarman Vural, A Enis Çetin, in International Conference on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing (ICASSP) 2018.
We present a non-Euclidean vector product for neural networks. Our vector product operator does not require any multiplications while providing correlation information between two vectors, unlike ordinary neurons which require inner products.


A sparse temporal mesh model for brain decoding.
Arman Afrasiyabi, Itır Önal Ertuğrul, Fatos T. Yarman Vural in the 15th International Conference on Cognitive Informatics & Cognitive Computing (ICCI*CC) 2016 hosted by Stanford University.
We propose Sparse Temporal Mesh Model (STMM), an architecture, for dimensionality reduction by combining the voxel selection methods with the mesh learning method.
